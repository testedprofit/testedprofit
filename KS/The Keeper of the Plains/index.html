The Keeper of the Plains

An iconic 44-foot Cor-Ten steel tribute to Indigenous peoples, standing on sacred ground at the confluence of the Big and Little Arkansas rivers.

Sculptor & Significance

Created by renowned Kiowa-Comanche artist Blackbear Bosin. Donated in 1974 for the U.S. Bicentennial.

The figure's raised hands symbolize a gesture of supplication to the Great Spirit, representing peace and goodwill for all.

Location & Access

Situated at the river confluence, adjacent to the Mid-America All-Indian Museum.

  • Free public access (5 a.m. - Midnight)
  • Accessible via "Bow and Arrow" pedestrian bridges

The Ring of Fire

Completed in 2007 as part of a $20 million beautification project. Fire drums on boulders illuminate the sculpture nightly for 15 minutes. The fires represent the sacred hoop of the four elements: earth, air, water, and fire.
