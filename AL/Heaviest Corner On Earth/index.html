Digital Landmark Profile

The Heaviest Corner
on Earth

A historic intersection at 20th Street and 1st Avenue North, Birmingham. Named for the nearly simultaneous construction of four of the South's tallest skyscrapers between 1902 and 1912.

Historic Status

Listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1985.

The intersection earned its nickname due to the immense weight of the steel and stone used to construct four massive skyscrapers on a single corner in just one decade.

Visiting Tips

  • 24/7 Access: Public street corner, no admission fee.
  • Marker: Bronze plaque located outside the Empire Building (Elyton Hotel).
  • Pro Tip: Visit the MoonShine rooftop bar at the Elyton Hotel for the best architectural views.
