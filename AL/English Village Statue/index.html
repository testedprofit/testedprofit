Civitas: The Spirit of Community

A bronze sculpture by James Barnhill standing as the gateway to English Village. A tribute to community strength and architectural heritage.

"Civitas"

Latin for "Citizens" or "Community."

The name reflects the tight-knit nature and community spirit of the English Village area in Mountain Brook, Alabama.

Making a Place

Evokes the memory of Carolyn Smith, the self-taught architect who designed many English Village buildings in the 1920s.

The figure is not a literal depiction but captures the spirit of creating a place. Designed to project strength over conventional beauty.

Gateway to the Village

Dedicated in 1998, this "gateway" piece depicts a female figure examining models of the village buildings. It serves as a focal point at the entrance of the commercial district, welcoming visitors to the European-inspired boutiques and restaurants.

"A prominent and symbolic landmark that helps define the unique character and history of English Village."

Location

Cahaba Road & Fairway Drive

Mountain Brook, AL

Artist

James Barnhill

Sculptor

Standing at the intersection of history and community, "Civitas" preserves the legacy of those who built English Village.
