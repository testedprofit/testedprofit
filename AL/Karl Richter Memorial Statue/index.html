Here Am I. Send Me.

The Karl Richter Memorial at Maxwell Air Force Base, honoring the youngest American pilot to shoot down a MiG in Vietnam.

198 Missions

1st Lt. Karl Richter was the first pilot to complete a 100-mission tour over North Vietnam, then immediately volunteered for a second.

On July 28, 1967, during his 198th mission, his F-105 was hit by anti-aircraft fire. He was only 24 years old.

Historic Air Park

Located on Chennault Circle within the Maxwell AFB Historic Air Park in Montgomery.

Surrounded by vintage aircraft displays

Base access required

A Beacon of Valor

The base of the statue bears the inscription from the Book of Isaiah: "Whom shall I send, and who will go for us?... Here am I. Send me." This monument stands as a daily inspiration to the airmen passing through Air University, symbolizing the ultimate standard of service and sacrifice.

"Dedicated June 13, 1992, to a true American hero."

1st Lieutenant
