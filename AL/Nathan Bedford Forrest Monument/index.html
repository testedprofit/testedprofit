Digital Landmark Profile

Nathan Bedford Forrest Monument

A controversial memorial located in the Confederate Memorial Circle within the Old Live Oak Cemetery in Selma, Alabama.

A Divisive Figure

Honors a Confederate General who was also a slave trader and the first Grand Wizard of the KKK.

Recognized by supporters as the "Defender of Selma" during the Civil War, but viewed by critics as a symbol of hate and oppression.

Old Live Oak Cemetery

Located at 110 W Dallas Ave in Selma.

Confederate Memorial Circle

Accessible during cemetery hours

History of Controversy

The monument's presence in Selma, a city synonymous with the Civil Rights Movement, highlights conflicting historical narratives in the South. Its journey from a city park to the cemetery has been marked by protests, theft, and heated public debate.

"A potent symbol and frequent subject of debate over historical memory and public spaces."

2000

Initial Erection

Placed in a city park; sparked immediate protests.

2001

Relocation

Moved to Old Live Oak Cemetery due to outcry.

2012

Theft

Bronze bust stolen; construction halted on replacement.

2015

Reinstallation

A new, larger bust was installed at the site.

Standing at the intersection of history and memory, a focal point of ongoing civil rights discussions.
