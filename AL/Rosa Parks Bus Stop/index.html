The Spark of a Movement

The historic marker in Court Square, Montgomery, where a single act of defiance by Rosa Parks changed the course of American history.

A Seat for Justice

On December 1, 1955, Rosa Parks refused to give up her seat to a white passenger.

Her arrest mobilized the community and launched the 381-day Montgomery Bus Boycott led by Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., leading to the desegregation of public transport.

Court Square

Situated in downtown Montgomery, near the intersection of Montgomery St. and Dexter Ave, across from the Court Square Fountain.

Marked by a bronze historical plaque

Free public access 24/7

Sacred Ground

The area surrounding the bus stop highlights the complex history of Montgomery. From its past as a major slave market to its central role in the fight for civil rights, this spot is part of the Selma to Montgomery National Historic Trail.

"A pivotal moment in American history, marked simply in the pavement."
