History & Background

Albert Patterson (1894–1954) was an Alabama attorney and Democratic Party nominee for attorney general in 1954 who was assassinated in Phenix City, Alabama just days after winning his primary election. His assassination was directly linked to his campaign to clean up Phenix City — then one of the most corrupt and crime-ridden cities in the United States. The city across the Chattahoochee River from Columbus, Georgia had been controlled for years by a powerful criminal syndicate that operated gambling halls, prostitution, and protection rackets with the complicity of local law enforcement and officials.

Patterson's assassination had dramatic consequences. His son, John Patterson, took his father's place on the ballot and won the general election. Alabama Governor Gordon Persons declared martial law in Phenix City and the National Guard moved in to shut down the criminal operations. The dramatic cleanup of Phenix City was documented in a 1955 film also titled "The Phenix City Story." John Patterson went on to become Alabama's governor (1959–1963), where he faced the momentous challenges of the Civil Rights Movement, initially taking a position opposing integration before gradually moderating his stance.

The Albert Patterson Statue honors a man who paid the ultimate price for standing against organized crime and political corruption. His story is a reminder that civic courage carries real risks, and that the struggles for good governance that seem abstract in textbooks were lived by real people in real communities. The Phenix City of the 1950s was a cautionary tale about what happens when criminal enterprise captures government — and the Patterson story is about ordinary citizens and honest officials who fought back against those conditions.

Nearby Attractions

National Infantry Museum (Columbus, GA)

Located across the river in Columbus, Georgia adjacent to Fort Moore (formerly Fort Benning), the National Infantry Museum is one of the finest military museums in the United States. The $100 million facility documents the history of American infantry from the Revolution to present day with exceptional artifacts, vehicles, and immersive exhibits. Free admission.

Chattahoochee Riverwalk (Columbus, GA)

The Columbus Riverwalk along the Chattahoochee River offers excellent urban outdoor recreation including whitewater kayaking on what is billed as the longest urban whitewater course in the world. The riverwalk connects multiple Columbus attractions and provides views across to the Alabama bank. The Iron Works Trade and Convention Center anchors the north end of the Phenix City, Alabama side.

Phenix City Historical Sites

Phenix City has worked to reclaim its history beyond the criminal era of the 1950s. The city sits along the Chattahoochee River with access to outdoor recreation and maintains historical markers documenting its complex past. The transformation of Phenix City from a national symbol of municipal corruption to a regular Alabama community is itself a significant historical story worth exploring.
