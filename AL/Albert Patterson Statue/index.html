Albert Patterson Statue

A life-size bronze monument in Phenix City, Alabama, honoring the attorney general-elect whose assassination sparked the cleanup of "Sin City."

Cleaning Up Sin City

Patterson ran on a platform to clean up Phenix City, known then for widespread illegal gambling and organized crime.

On June 18, 1954, he was fatally shot near his law office, mere steps from where this statue now stands.

A Quiet Reflection

Created by artist John Lumpkin.

Depicts Patterson holding a cane & apple

Invites passersby to sit on the bench

Good Triumph Over Evil

Patterson's murder was the tipping point. It led to the declaration of martial law and the complete dismantlement of organized crime in Phenix City. His legacy continued through his son, John Patterson, who was elected attorney general in his wake and later became Governor.

"A reminder of the power of one person to create change."
