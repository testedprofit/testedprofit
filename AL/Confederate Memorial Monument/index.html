Confederate Memorial Monument

An 88-foot obelisk standing on the north lawn of the Alabama State Capitol, dedicated to the 122,000 Alabamians who served in the Civil War.

Stone & Bronze

Designed by Alexander Doyle and dedicated in 1898. Features four granite statues representing military branches and a bronze figure of "Patriotism" at the top.

Cornerstone laid by Jefferson Davis in 1886.

A Contested Space

Located at 600 Dexter Ave, a site often linked to the "Lost Cause" ideology.

Flags removed in 2015 by Gov. Bentley

Erected during the Jim Crow era

In the Shadow of History

The monument sits in a complex historical landscape, situated on the Capitol grounds near the Dexter Avenue King Memorial Baptist Church and the Civil Rights Memorial.
