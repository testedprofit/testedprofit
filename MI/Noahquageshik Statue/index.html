Grand Valley State University

THE
PEACEMAKER.

Standing seven feet tall in bronze, Noahquageshik (Chief Noonday) watches over the Grand River, honoring the Anishinaabe leader who bridged cultures in Grand Rapids.

The Leader

Noahquageshik ("The One Who Hangs in the Sky") led the Grand River Ottawa. He was pivotal in allowing Baptist missionaries to establish schools, seeking education and adaptation for his people.

The Artist

Created by sculptor Jason Quigno, himself a descendant of the Saginaw Chippewa Tribe. The 7-foot bronze masterpiece blends traditional storytelling with contemporary form.

The Setting

Located at the John C. Kennedy Hall of Engineering. The placement symbolizes the bridge between indigenous knowledge of the land and modern engineering practices.
