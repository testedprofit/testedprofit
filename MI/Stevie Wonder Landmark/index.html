THE ROOTS OF
RHYTHM.

Before the 25 Grammys and the induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, there was a quiet street in Saginaw, Michigan. The Stevie Wonder Landmark marks the literal ground where musical history began.

THE WONDER ROCK

A permanent commemorative bronze plaque mounted on a massive natural boulder. Known by locals as the "Wonder Rock," it serves as an immovable anchor for the community's heritage.

Symbol of Resilience

"THE BIG HOUSE"

Located at the former site of a local boarding house frequented by Stevie's family. It represents the specific urban fabric that nurtured one of the world's greatest creative minds.

Geographic Origin

COMMUNITY ROOTS

Dedicated in 2005 by historian Lula Briggs-Galloway. Today, it stands as a focal point for Juneteenth celebrations and cultural education in mid-Michigan.
