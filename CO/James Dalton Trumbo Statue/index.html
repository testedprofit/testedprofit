Historical Monument

James Dalton Trumbo Statue

A distinctive bronze sculpture on Main Street honoring the Academy Award-winning screenwriter and novelist.

The James Dalton Trumbo Statue is a prominent public art installation located on Main Street in Grand Junction, Colorado, directly in front of the historic Avalon Theatre. Dedicated in 2007, the sculpture pays tribute to one of the city's most famous and controversial former residents, screenwriter Dalton Trumbo, who grew up in the region before achieving Hollywood fame.

The installation is the inaugural piece in the "Legends of the Grand Valley" project, a series designed to highlight individuals who have significantly shaped local history. The monument symbolizes a moment of reconciliation between the community and Trumbo, who had a complex relationship with his hometown following the publication of his critical novel, Eclipse.

Today, the statue serves as a cultural landmark for residents, tourists, and film enthusiasts. It provides a tangible connection to Hollywood history and the McCarthy era blacklist, while also celebrating the artistic achievements of a Grand Junction High School graduate who won two Academy Awards.

Key Facts

  • Location: 645 Main St, Grand Junction, CO
  • Artist: J. Michael Wilson
  • Dedication: October 13, 2007
  • Subject: James Dalton Trumbo
  • Material: Bronze
  • Project: Legends of the Grand Valley (Inaugural Piece)

Notable Features & Attributes

The Bathtub Composition

Based on a famous 1967 photograph, the bronze sculpture depicts Trumbo working in a bathtubâ€”an eccentric habit he adopted to relieve back pain while writing.

Historical Accuracy

The detailed work includes period-specific accessories, including a pen in hand, a cigarette, and a cup of coffee, capturing the writer's intense work environment.

Symbol of Reconciliation

The installation marks the community's acceptance of its "not-so-favorite son," whose 1935 novel Eclipse had previously alienated many locals with its unflattering portrayals.

Cinema Context

Positioned directly in front of the Avalon Theatre, the location underscores Trumbo's massive contribution to cinema, including classics like Spartacus and Roman Holiday.

Visitor Information

Accessibility & Viewing The statue is located on the public sidewalk at 645 Main Street. It is fully accessible to pedestrians and wheelchair users 24 hours a day.

Historical Context Trumbo was one of the "Hollywood Ten" blacklisted during the McCarthy era. Visitors can learn more about his local history at the Mesa County Public Library, which supported the 2005 reprinting of his novel Eclipse.

Nearby Amenities The sculpture's location in downtown Grand Junction places it within walking distance of numerous restaurants, shops, and the historic Avalon Theatre, making it a convenient stop on any downtown tour.

Local Context & Connections

The statue is a key component of the city's "Art on the Corner" program and the specific "Legends of the Grand Valley" series.
