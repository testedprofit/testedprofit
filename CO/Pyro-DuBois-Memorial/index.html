Colorado Military Memorial

Capt. William H.
"Pyro" DuBois Jr.
Memorial

A lasting tribute to a distinguished military officer whose courage and service left an enduring mark on Colorado and the nation.

History & Background

Captain William H. DuBois Jr. — known widely by his nickname "Pyro" — was a military figure whose life of service became the basis for this Colorado memorial. The nickname "Pyro" was earned through his expertise and fearless approach to his duties, a reflection of both his personality and his professional reputation among peers and those he served with.

Military memorials like this one play an important role in preserving the stories of individuals who served their country and their communities. Colorado has a strong tradition of honoring veterans and service members through public monuments, and the DuBois memorial continues that tradition — ensuring that the memory of Captain DuBois is not lost to history.

The memorial serves as both a tribute to Captain DuBois personally and as a broader symbol of the sacrifices made by military personnel from Colorado across generations of service to the United States.

Military Legacy

The rank of Captain carries with it a long tradition of leadership responsibility. Captains in the United States military are charged with the direct command of personnel, making decisions under pressure and setting the standard for those in their charge. Captain DuBois embodied these qualities throughout his career.

Colorado has produced many distinguished military officers, and memorials dedicated to figures like Captain DuBois help connect current and future generations to that heritage. The "Jr." designation in his name indicates a family tradition of service — a lineage of commitment that the memorial honors alongside the individual.

His distinctive nickname speaks to the informal camaraderie of military life, where personalities are recognized and preserved through the language of those who served alongside them. That "Pyro" became the name most associated with Captain DuBois is itself a piece of living military history.

Visitor Information

Location

Colorado, United States. The memorial is located at a publicly accessible site — check local Colorado veterans' organization listings for current directions.

Hours

Publicly accessible during daylight hours. No admission fee. Outdoor memorial — dress appropriately for Colorado weather conditions.

Getting There

Street parking typically available nearby. The site is accessible on foot. Respectful, quiet visitation is encouraged at all memorial sites.

  • Colorado Veterans Monument
