Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial

A Symbol of Freedom, Justice, and Hope in the Heart of Denver's City Park

About the Memorial

The Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial in Denver is a powerful tribute to one of history's most influential civil rights leaders. Located in the beautiful City Park, this monument, created by renowned sculptor Ed Dwight, serves as a beacon of inspiration, reminding us of the enduring fight for equality and justice. It's more than just a statue; it's a profound visual narrative of the African American struggle for freedom and the universal principles Dr. King championed.

The Memorial's Profound Design

Standing on the Shoulders of Giants

At the heart of the memorial stands a monumental bronze sculpture of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Symbolically, he is depicted standing on the shoulders of four pivotal historical figures who profoundly influenced his philosophy of nonviolent resistance and his pursuit of civil rights. This visual metaphor powerfully conveys the cumulative effort of generations in the fight for freedom.

Dr. King's Vision

A beacon of hope and justice for all.

A Chronological Journey: The Pedestal and Panels

The pedestal supporting Dr. King, along with four surrounding bas-relief panels, chronicles the profound historical narrative of the African American struggle for freedom and civil rights, interwoven with Dr. King's timeless quotations.

Bottom Tier: Nobel Peace Prize

Honors Dr. King's 1964 Nobel Peace Prize. Side panels depict the struggle from 1619 through 1960, marking the beginning of the African American journey in America.

Panel 1: Slavery to Freedom

Illustrates the arduous journey from the chains of slavery towards the promise of liberation.

Panel 2: Civil Rights Struggle

Highlights pivotal events and landmark legislation of the Civil Rights Movement, showcasing the fight for equal rights.

Panel 3: Justice & Quotes

Features Lady Justice and powerful engravings of Dr. King's most impactful quotes on the pursuit of fairness and equity.

Panel 4: Living the Dream

Focuses on the present and future, embodying themes of love, hope, and truth in the ongoing realization of Dr. King's vision.

The Denver MLK "Marade"

The Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial is the sacred starting point for Denver's annual "Marade" (a blend of "march" and "parade"). This vibrant event is one of the largest and most significant celebrations of MLK Day in the entire United States, drawing tens of thousands of participants each January.

The "Marade" embodies Dr. King's principles of nonviolent action and community engagement. It's a day when people from all walks of life come together to honor his enduring legacy, reflect on the progress made, and renew their commitment to social justice.

Spearheaded by the tireless efforts of former State Representative Wilma Webb, who helped make MLK Day a state holiday in Colorado in 1984, the Marade has become a powerful tradition in Denver.

The Visionary Artist: Ed Dwight

The Denver Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial was created by the acclaimed Denver-based sculptor Ed Dwight. A true pioneer, Dwight was not only a gifted artist but also the first African American astronaut candidate selected by NASA in 1961.

After his career as an aerospace engineer and pilot, Dwight transitioned to art, focusing on sculptures that powerfully narrate African American history and celebrate its pioneers. He has created thousands of sculptures, with over 130 public memorials across the country. His deep understanding of history and his artistic mastery are evident in the profound symbolism and emotional resonance of the Denver MLK Memorial.

Ed Dwight: Artist & Pioneer

Resilience and Rededication

In February 2024, the memorial faced an unfortunate act of vandalism, with several bronze pieces stolen. This incident caused profound distress, particularly for the artist Ed Dwight and the community.

However, thanks to the swift recovery of the stolen pieces and dedicated efforts, the memorial was repaired and proudly rededicated in January 2025. This event underscored the community's unwavering commitment to Dr. King's legacy and the memorial's symbolic importance, demonstrating its resilience in the face of adversity.
