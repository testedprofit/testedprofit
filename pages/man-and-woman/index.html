Public Art

Man and Woman

Iconic bronze sculptures by Fernando Botero standing guard at the Denver Performing Arts Complex.

"Man and Woman" is a celebrated pair of bronze sculptures located within the high-glass galleria of the Denver Performing Arts Complex (DPAC). Created by the internationally renowned Colombian artist Fernando Botero, these figures stand as prominent cultural sentinels in the heart of Denver's theater district.

Installed in 1998, the sculptures exemplify Botero's signature style, often referred to as "Boterismo." This aesthetic is characterized by exaggerated volume and rotund proportions, lending the figures a sense of monumentality, stability, and subtle humor. The man and woman stand separately but are unified in style and presence, creating a silent dialogue across the galleria floor.

The artwork serves as a central meeting point for theatergoers attending performances at the Temple Hoyne Buell Theatre or Ellie Caulkins Opera House. As part of the City and County of Denver's public art collection, "Man and Woman" represents the city's commitment to integrating world-class art into its civic spaces.

Key Facts

  • Location: 998 14th St, Denver, CO 80202
  • Artist: Fernando Botero
  • Installed: 1998
  • Height: Approx. 13 feet
  • Material: Cast Bronze
  • Setting: Denver Performing Arts Complex Galleria

Notable Features & Attributes

Signature "Boterismo"

The figures display Botero's trademark volumetric style. By exaggerating the size and roundness of the subjects, the artist explores the sensuality of form and volume in three-dimensional space.

Imposing Scale

Standing roughly 13 feet tall, the sculptures command the large, open space of the galleria. Their height allows them to hold their own against the towering glass roof and surrounding architectural structures.

Interactive Placement

Placed at ground level without high pedestals, the artwork invites interaction. Theater patrons frequently gather around the base, using the statues as a recognizable rendezvous point.

Enduring Material

Cast in bronze, the figures possess a timeless durability. The dark patina of the metal contrasts sharply with the light, airy atmosphere of the glass-enclosed galleria.

Visitor Information

Access & Location The sculptures are located within the open-air galleria of the Denver Performing Arts Complex at 14th and Curtis Streets. The area is publicly accessible 24 hours a day, though it is liveliest during showtimes.

Parking The DPAC garage provides direct access to the galleria. Visitors can park and take the elevator up to the main level, emerging directly near the sculptures.

Photography "Man and Woman" are among the most photographed landmarks in the complex. The natural light filtering through the galleria roof makes daytime photography particularly effective.

Local Context & Connections

The artwork anchors the cultural hub of downtown Denver, connecting various performance venues.
