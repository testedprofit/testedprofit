Historical Landmark

The Newsboy Statue

A bronze tribute to the spirit of enterprise and the history of journalism in the heart of Golden, Colorado.

The Newsboy Statue is a celebrated bronze sculpture located at 1020 11th Street in Golden, Colorado. Standing as a pivotal piece of the city's public art collection, this evocative figure captures the raw spirit and determination of the young newspaper carriers who were once the lifeblood of American communication.

Strategically placed near the start of the Clear Creek Trail, the statue enhances the scenic beauty of the downtown corridor while serving as a poignant historical marker. Its dedication was timed to coincide with the 138th birthday of the Golden Transcript, honoring the enduring legacy of local journalism that has shaped the community's identity for over a century.

As a tangible link between Golden's vibrant present and its rich Western past, the statue invites visitors to reflect on the era when news traveled through the hands of dedicated individuals on street corners, rather than through digital signals.

Key Facts

  • Location: 1020 11th St, Golden, CO 80401
  • Material: Bronze Sculpture
  • Subject: Historical Newsboy
  • Dedication: 138th Birthday of Golden Transcript
  • Program: Golden Public Art Initiative
  • Access: Public (24/7)

Symbolism & Features

Spirit of Enterprise

The figure symbolizes the grit and responsibility of young entrepreneurs. Newsboys were often the first to learn the value of commerce and customer service in a rapidly developing nation.

Power of Information

By commemorating the distribution of newspapers, the statue celebrates the vital role of the press in a functioning democracy and community cohesion.

Artistic Integration

This piece is part of a broader initiative formally established in 2013 by the Golden Civic Foundation to integrate art into public spaces, fostering a unique cultural identity.

Nostalgic Connection

The bronze medium and period-accurate clothing serve as a visual bridge to the past, reminding modern visitors of the city's humble beginnings.

Visitor Information

Access & Location The statue is located at 1020 11th St, on the west side of Washington Avenue, just north of Clear Creek. It is easily accessible from the sidewalk and serves as a popular meeting point for runners and cyclists using the adjacent trail.

Trail Connection The site marks a key access point to the Clear Creek Trail system, making it an ideal start or end point for a recreational walk through Golden's riverfront parks.

Nearby Amenities Being in downtown Golden, the statue is steps away from local cafes, the Golden History Museum, and the scenic bridge crossings over Clear Creek.

Local Context & Connections

The Newsboy Statue anchors the cultural and historical narrative of downtown Golden.

Related Institution Golden Transcript Newspaper Civic Partner Golden Civic Foundation

The Newsboy Statue is a permanent bronze public art installation located at 1020 11th St in Golden, Colorado. Commemorating the history of the Golden Transcript and the era of street-corner news distribution, the sculpture serves as a cultural landmark at the entrance to the Clear Creek Trail system. It remains a focal point for civic pride and historical preservation in the city.
