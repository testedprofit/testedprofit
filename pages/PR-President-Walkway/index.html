Walkway of the Presidents

Paseo de los Presidentes · San Juan, Puerto Rico

A living monument honoring U.S. presidents who visited Puerto Rico during their time in office, located along the south side of the Capitol building.

Established in 2010, the Walkway of the Presidents is a linear park that commemorates the historical ties and complex relationship between the United States and Puerto Rico. The path honors presidents whose visits left a lasting imprint on the island’s political and cultural history.

Featured Presidential Statues

Theodore Roosevelt

First sitting president to visit Puerto Rico in 1906, depicted with one hand resting on his right side.

Franklin D. Roosevelt

One of only three statues worldwide showing him in a wheelchair, accompanied by his dog, Fala.

John F. Kennedy

Commemorating his 1961 visit, shown with an open coat and a hand at his waist.

Barack Obama

The most recent addition, added after his official visit to Puerto Rico in 2011.

Other Featured Presidents

Herbert Hoover (holding a hat), Harry S. Truman (hand in pocket), Dwight D. Eisenhower (saluting), Lyndon B. Johnson (standing tall), and Gerald Ford (carrying a pipe).

Visitor Information

Accessibility

Fully wheelchair accessible and located along Avenida de la Constitución, ideal for walking tours near Old San Juan.

Educational Experience

Each statue includes a bilingual plaque with historical context and details about each presidential visit.

Nearby Landmarks

Surrounded by the Monumento de la Recordación and the Holocaust Memorial.
