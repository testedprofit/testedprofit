Golden, Colorado

The Ancient Guardians

Witness the vibrant resurrection of the Triceratops through the visionary metalwork of artist Pat Madison.

A Vision in Metal & Color

In a striking fusion of paleontology and industrial art, local sculptor Pat Madison has summoned a herd of giants from the past. Using electrical conduit piping, painted wood, and reclaimed plastics, Madison constructs forms that are both skeletal and vibrantly alive.

The installation features a family unit—a towering "dad," two "moms," and a playful "baby"—standing sentinel against the Colorado winds. The vivid coloration is a deliberate choice, breaking away from the drab greys of fossils to reimagine the prehistoric world in brilliant, living technicolor.

The Triceratops Connection

Golden, Colorado is not just a backdrop; it is hallowed ground for these creatures. During the late Cretaceous period (68–66 million years ago), this region teemed with Triceratops.

  • The Three-Horned Face: Known for skulls reaching 8 feet in length.
  • The Frill: Likely used for defense, temperature regulation, and mating displays.
  • Local Heritage: Some of the earliest Triceratops fossils were unearthed right here in this vicinity.

Meet the Artist: Pat Madison

Pat Madison is a sculptor defined by his connection to the Western landscape. His work transcends mere representation, acting as a bridge between the industrial present and the biological past.

Beyond these prehistoric guardians, Madison's portfolio enriches Golden's outdoor gallery with pieces like the "Return of the Cutthroat" fish sculptures and the whimsical "Rotary Butterflies." His art is a subtle yet powerful call to conservation, inviting viewers to protect the biodiversity that remains.

Visiting the Guardians

Located near Highway 93 and West 56th Avenue, north of Golden.

Note: The sculptures reside on private land owned by Wildlife Protection Solutions. Please view and photograph safely from the roadside or designated pull-offs. Do not cross the fence line.
