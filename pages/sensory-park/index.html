A Haven of Inclusivity

Sensory Park is more than just a playground; it's a testament to community commitment, providing a rich and accessible play experience for children of all abilities. Located in Westminster, Colorado, it stands as a shining example of inclusive public spaces.

With over 17 years of service to families, and significant involvement from Children's Hospital in its early funding, Sensory Park pioneered accessible play in the Denver metropolitan area, making it a beloved community asset.

Unforgettable Features for Every Child

100% Accessibility

One of the only fully accessible parks in Denver, with ramps integrated throughout play structures, ensuring every area is reachable for mobility device users.

Adaptive Swings

Enjoy specially designed adaptive and supportive swings, allowing children of all physical abilities to experience the joy of swinging.

Musical Delights

Engage auditory senses with various musical instruments, including drums and xylophones, for interactive sound play.

Cool Water Play

A dedicated water play area provides refreshing fun and sensory exploration, especially on warmer days.

Unique Static-Free Slide

Experience a one-of-a-kind slide designed to eliminate static electricity, a crucial benefit for children with cochlear implants.

Scenic Views & Trails

Enjoy pleasant mountain views and direct access to the adjacent Walnut Creek Open Space, perfect for extending your outdoor adventure.

Plan Your Visit

Cost to Visit: Absolutely FREE!

As a public park, Sensory Park is completely free for all visitors to enjoy. No tickets, no fees, just endless fun.

Construction & Renovation

While its original construction cost is not readily available, Sensory Park has received significant investments for improvements. In 2019, an Adams County Open Space Grant of $375,000 combined with $250,000 from the Conservation Trust Fund budget, totaled $625,000 for vital upgrades including equipment replacement, surface improvements, and new entryway arches.

The park has served the community for over 17 years, with many sources indicating its popularity and initial funding/creation around 1998, making it one of the very first accessible playgrounds in the Denver area.

Park Amenities & Important Notes

  • Playground equipment for diverse play
  • Covered picnic shelter and tables for family gatherings
  • Convenient parking lot
  • Drinking fountain available
  • Important Note: Some sources indicate a lack of public restrooms. Please plan accordingly for your visit.
