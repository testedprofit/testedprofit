Origin & Design

A Gesture of Solidarity

Commissioned in 2003 by Chris Sanaee, an Iranian immigrant and local property owner, this landmark stands as a permanent gesture of solidarity with the United States following the 9/11 attacks.

The Structure

Standing on a 10-foot round pedestal, the statue itself reaches approximately 40â€“50 feet. It features a classic eagle emblem on the base and a plaque sharing a message of freedom.

Quirky History

The "Fire" Incident

During original assembly, a welding error famously caused the statue's head to catch fire. The damage was significant enough that a completely new head had to be ordered.

Visitor Experience

Hidden Location

Tucked behind a strip mall at 4287 SE Roethe Rd. It's an unexpected find near businesses like Arrivederci Wine & Jazz.

Accessibility

The site is a free roadside attraction, fully accessible from the parking lot, and remains a permanent fixture of the Milwaukie landscape.
