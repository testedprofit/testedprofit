The Newsboy Statue

A bronze tribute to the spirit of enterprise in the heart of Golden, Colorado.

An Iconic Golden Landmark

The Newsboy Statue is a celebrated bronze sculpture and a pivotal piece of the public art collection in Golden, Colorado. This evocative statue stands as a tribute to a bygone era, capturing the raw spirit and determination of the young newspaper carriers who were once a cornerstone of American communication.

Located prominently in the heart of the city, the statue not only enhances the scenic beauty of Golden but also serves as a poignant historical marker. It connects the city's vibrant present with its rich Western past, creating a tangible link to the history of journalism and community development in the region.


Historical Significance

The placement and dedication of The Newsboy Statue are deeply intertwined with the history of Golden and its institutions. It represents a confluence of civic pride, historical preservation, and artistic expression.

Connection to the Golden Transcript

The statue's dedication was meaningfully timed to coincide with the 138th birthday of the Golden Transcript, the city's long-standing local newspaper. This gesture honors the enduring legacy of local journalism and the critical role the newspaper has played in shaping the community's identity and keeping its citizens informed for over a century.

Golden's Public Art Program

This landmark is a testament to Golden's commitment to public art. The initiative, which began informally in the 1990s through the efforts of the Golden Civic Foundation, was formally established in 2013 by Mayor Jacob Smith. The program aims to integrate art into public spaces, fostering a unique cultural identity and enriching the lives of residents and visitors alike.


The Statue's Symbolism

Beyond its local importance, The Newsboy Statue resonates with broader American historical themes. It symbolizes several key ideas:

  • The Spirit of Enterprise: Newsboys were often young entrepreneurs, demonstrating grit, responsibility, and an early understanding of commerce.
  • The Power of Information: The statue celebrates the entire newspaper industry and its vital role in a functioning democracy.
  • A Nostalgic Link to the Past: It serves as a reminder of a time when news traveled not by digital signals, but through the hands of dedicated individuals on street corners.
