The Majestic Eagle Sculpture

Unveiling the symbolism behind public eagle statues in Lakewood, CO

It appears there's some confusion in public records. The prominent "Eagle" sculpture by Joseph Nicolosi often discussed in search results is located atop the Denver City and County Building, not here at the Denver West Plaza in Lakewood.

Unfortunately, specific detailed information about the sculptor and the precise commissioning history of the Eagle statue at 1400 Denver West-Marriott Blvd, Lakewood, CO 80401 is not readily available in public art archives.

Many public art pieces in private developments don't always have their full histories as widely documented as city-commissioned public art. However, we can speak to the general symbolism of eagle statues in public spaces:

Symbolism in Public Art

  • ✓ Freedom and Liberty As a bird that soars high and is not easily constrained, the eagle embodies ideals of freedom and independence.
  • ✓ Strength and Power Their predatory nature and commanding presence in the sky often symbolize strength, courage, and dominance.
  • ✓ Vision and Foresight Eagles are known for their keen eyesight, leading to associations with clear vision, foresight, and looking towards the future.
  • ✓ Patriotism and National Identity In the U.S., the bald eagle is the national bird and a strong emblem of patriotism, representing the nation's values and spirit.
  • ✓ Protection and Guardianship Their watchful nature can also symbolize guardianship and protection.

It's highly probable that the Eagle statue at Denver West Plaza was commissioned by the developers or property owners of the plaza to evoke these positive and aspirational qualities, contributing to the overall aesthetic and symbolic message of the commercial space. While the specific artist and exact intent remain undocumented in accessible records, its presence likely serves to instill a sense of strength, freedom, and prominence in the area.
