The Overview

Located at 229 S 3rd St, Hayden Town Park functions not as a wild nature reserve, but as a vital civic infrastructure. It provides essential open space for community cohesion, local events like "Hayden Daze," and casual recreation.

For the conservation-minded, this park serves as an accessible entry point to the outdoors before venturing to the nearby Carpenter Ranch Preserve.

Park Rules

  • Dogs allowed (Leash)
  • Dawn to Dusk
  • No Camping

Tennis Courts

Two well-maintained hard-surface courts available for public use. Ideal for recreational matches.

Community Lawn

Expansive green space utilized for local festivals, picnics, and the "Ride the Cog" gravel staging.

Accessibility

Wheelchair accessible pathways and parking ensure the park is usable by all members of the community.

Proximity

While urban, the park sits moments away from the Yampa River corridor and conservation easements.

The Carpenter Connection

While Hayden Town Park offers recreation, true conservationists should look just east to the Carpenter Ranch Preserve. Managed by The Nature Conservancy, it is a working model of how agriculture and riparian forest habitat protection can coexist along the Yampa River.
