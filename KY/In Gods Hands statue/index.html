Religious Memorial

In God's Hands Statue

A central bronze monument located in the Holy Innocents Section of Calvary Cemetery.

The "In God's Hands" statue is the focal point of the Holy Innocents Section within Calvary Cemetery in Louisville, Kentucky. Dedicated in June 2010 by Archbishop Joseph E. Kurtz, the monument anchors a dedicated garden space designed specifically for the burial, prayer, and reflection of families who have lost infants or children.

The bronze sculpture depicts the large hand of God gently cradling a small infant, offering a visual representation of comfort and spiritual safety. This imagery serves as a cornerstone for the surrounding garden, which functions as a place of solace for the grieving community.

Surrounding the central figure are over 1,500 granite pavers. These stones can be purchased to memorialize children buried either at Calvary or other cemeteries, creating a collective space of remembrance. The site is a significant spiritual landmark within the Archdiocesan Catholic Cemeteries of Louisville.

Key Facts

  • Location: 1600 Newburg Rd, Louisville, KY 40205
  • Dedication: June 2010
  • Facility: Calvary Cemetery
  • Section: Holy Innocents / In God's Hand Garden
  • Material: Bronze
  • Primary Access: Seasonal Daylight Hours

Notable Features & Attributes

Symbolic Imagery

The primary sculpture portrays a protective hand holding an infant, a design chosen to convey safety, peace, and divine care for the young lives memorialized there.

Memorial Pavers

Over 1,500 granite pavers surround the statue. These markers provide families a tangible way to honor children, regardless of where the actual burial took place.

Holy Innocents Section

The garden is part of a specialized section of the cemetery dedicated exclusively to infants and children, ensuring a quiet and respectful environment for mourning.

Infant Prayer Service

The site hosts a monthly prayer service, fostering a sense of community support for grieving families and offering a structured time for reflection.

Visitor Information

Seasonal Hours The cemetery gates operate on a seasonal schedule. From April 1 to September 30, hours are 8:00 AM – 7:00 PM. From October 1 to March 31, hours are 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM.

Access The garden is located within Calvary Cemetery at 1600 Newburg Rd. The area is accessible by car, with parking available on the cemetery roads. Pathways are paved for wheelchair access.

Atmosphere Visitors are reminded that this is an active cemetery and a place of deep personal significance for many families. Respectful conduct is expected at all times.

Local Context & Connections

Calvary Cemetery is the largest of the Archdiocesan Catholic Cemeteries in Louisville, serving as a primary historical and religious landmark in the Highlands area.
