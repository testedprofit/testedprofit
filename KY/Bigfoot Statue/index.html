Bigfoot Statue

A 14-foot hand-carved wooden sculpture located on the trails of Armco Park in Ashland, Kentucky.

The Bigfoot Statue in Armco Park is a whimsical and imposing piece of public art situated in the woodlands of Ashland, Kentucky. Standing 14 feet tall, the massive sculpture depicts the legendary Sasquatch and was meticulously hand-carved from wood by local artist Travis Williams.

The project originated from a local anecdote involving a mistaken "bear sighting" in the park, which Williams initially proposed commemorating with a bear carving. The concept evolved into a larger-than-life Bigfoot, transforming a local myth into a permanent artistic fixture. The creation process required approximately 40 hours of chainsaw carving over the course of a week.

Positioned along a park trail, the statue serves as a popular destination for hikers, families, and folklore enthusiasts seeking a unique photo opportunity. It represents a creative collaboration between local talent and the parks department to enhance the recreational experience of Armco Park.

Key Facts

  • Location: 1119 Armco Park, Ashland, KY 41102
  • Artist: Travis Williams
  • Height: 14 Feet
  • Material: Wood (Chainsaw Carving)
  • Admission: Free
  • Access: Open 24 Hours

Notable Features & Attributes

Monumental Scale

At 14 feet tall, the statue dominates the trailside setting, offering an impressive sense of scale that mimics the legendary stature of Sasquatch.

Chainsaw Artistry

Created by Boyd County artist Travis Williams, the piece demonstrates the skill of chainsaw carving, transforming raw timber into a detailed figure over 40 hours.

Local Folklore Origin

The inspiration for the statue is rooted in a local event where a "bear sighting" turned out to be a log, sparking the creative idea to place a permanent "creature" in the woods.

Natural Integration

Located within a wooded area off a trail, the wooden material and subject matter blend seamlessly with the forest environment of Armco Park.

Visitor Information

Access & Hours Armco Park is located at 1119 Armco Park, Ashland, KY. The park grounds and the trail leading to the statue are open to the public 24 hours a day with free admission.

Finding the Statue The sculpture is situated off a designated trail within the park's wooded section. Visitors should look for trail markers or inquire with park staff for the specific trailhead location.

Future Additions The artist has expressed plans to add more wooden creatures to the forest in the future, potentially expanding the site into a broader sculpture trail.

Local Context & Connections

The Bigfoot Statue highlights the intersection of outdoor recreation and local art in Boyd County.
