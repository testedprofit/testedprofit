Civic Monument

Benjamin Franklin Statue

A prominent bronze sculpture by Felix de Weldon adorning the entrance of the Louisville Free Public Library.

The Benjamin Franklin Statue is a distinguished public artwork located at the entrance of the Louisville Free Public Library's Main Branch on Curran Way. Installed in 1973, the larger-than-life bronze sculpture was created by Felix de Weldon, the artist best known for sculpting the Marine Corps War Memorial (Iwo Jima) in Arlington, Virginia.

The monument depicts Founding Father Benjamin Franklin seated on a bench in a moment of contemplation. Positioned facing the library, the figure serves as a symbolic guardian of knowledge, referencing Franklin's pivotal role in establishing the American lending library system, as well as his contributions to science and diplomacy.

As a focal point of the civic plaza, the statue offers visitors a moment of historical connection before entering the library. Its accessible design and open location make it a frequent stop for pedestrians, students, and tourists exploring downtown Louisville's architectural and cultural landmarks.

Notable Features & Attributes

Renowned Sculptor

The piece was crafted by Felix de Weldon, whose prolific career includes the iconic Iwo Jima Memorial, adding significant artistic pedigree to the installation.

Symbolic Attributes

Franklin holds a cane and a tablet inscribed with the "Treaty of Alliance" in English and French, highlighting his crucial role as a diplomat in securing support for the American Revolution.

Pedestal Inscription

The base bears a laudatory epitaph: "Venerated for benevolence, admired for talent, esteemed for patriotism, beloved for philanthropy," summarizing his multifaceted legacy.

Contextual Orientation

Uniquely positioned to face the library entrance rather than the street, the statue emphasizes Franklin's intellectual contribution to public education over his political fame.

Visitor Information

Access & Hours The statue is located outdoors at 12 Curran Way, adjacent to the library at 301 York St. It is open to the public 24 hours a day with no admission fee.

Accessibility The plaza surrounding the monument is paved and fully accessible to wheelchairs and strollers. The site is well-lit, making it safe for viewing in the evening.

Nearby Amenities Visitors can easily access the Louisville Free Public Library Main Branch during operating hours for restrooms, information, and further historical research.

Local Context & Connections

The statue integrates the history of American independence with Louisville's commitment to public literacy.
